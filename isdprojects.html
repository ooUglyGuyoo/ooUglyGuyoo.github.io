Design Thinking

ISD teach me to implement design thinking in all projects. Design thinking is an iterative process in which you seek to understand your users, challenge assumptions, redefine problems and create innovative solutions which you can prototype and test. The overall goal is to identify alternative strategies and solutions that are not instantly apparent with your initial level of understanding.

Project Process

Usually, a typical ISD project follows these processes:

  1. Topic selection and scoping

    2. The topic is scoped based on either professor's requirement or industrial partners.

  2. Empathize

    3. Based on the topic selected, we will do shadowing, interviews, observations, studies or investigation on our potential target users

  3. Define and redefine the problems

    4. Usually, one cannot identify the problems right, that is why we need to find the RIGHT problem by empathizing. We might need to do this for several time until we think we found the right problem that is worth solving

  4. Ideation

    5. To find the best solution to solve the problem we do literature review, brainstorming and technology research.

  5. Prototyping

    6. Functional prototype (Proof of Concept) will be built to implement our solution idea.

  6. Testing

    7. Tests will be done on the prototype and several prototypes and tests might need to be done to reach our expectation.
